# Data retention and your rights

This document explains what data we collect, why we keep it, and what happens when you delete your account. It is written in plain language, not legal jargon.

## What data we collect and why

- **Account information** — Your name, email address, and profile image (if you set one). We use this to identify you in the app, show your name on comments and activity, and contact you about your account (e.g. password reset).
- **Documents and files** — The documents you upload and their content. We store these so you can edit, share, and collaborate on them.
- **Comments** — Comments you leave on your own or others’ documents. We keep these so that document owners and collaborators can see the conversation.
- **Activity and notifications** — Records of actions (e.g. “X shared this document”) and notifications we send you (e.g. new comments). We use these to power the activity feed and in-app notifications.
- **Sharing and invitations** — Who you’ve invited to documents, and invitations you’ve accepted. We need this to enforce access and show you documents you’ve been invited to.
- **Sessions and sign-in** — Sign-in sessions and, if you use OAuth, linked accounts (e.g. Google). We use these to keep you logged in and to support “Sign in with Google” (or other providers).
- **Technical and security data** — Things like password-reset tokens and rate-limit data. We use these to secure your account and prevent abuse.

We do not sell your personal data.

## How long we keep data

- **While your account is active** — We keep the data above for as long as you use the service, so the product works correctly.
- **After you request deletion** — Your account is marked for deletion and scheduled for permanent removal. After that date, we run a process that erases or anonymizes your data (see below). We do not keep your personal data longer than needed for this process.

## What happens when you delete your account

When you request account deletion:

1. **Your account** — Your user record (name, email, profile, etc.) is permanently deleted.
2. **Your documents and files** — All documents you own and their files in storage are deleted.
3. **Your comments on others’ documents** — Your name is removed and replaced with “Deleted User” so the conversation history stays, but it is no longer tied to you.
4. **Activity and notifications** — Activity entries that referred to you are anonymized (we keep the fact that something happened, but not that it was you). All notifications to you or triggered by you are deleted.
5. **Invitations** — Invitations you sent are deleted. Invitations you received are anonymized (your email and link to your account are removed).
6. **Other records** — We delete or anonymize: document viewers, guest records tied to your email, password-reset tokens, OAuth accounts, and any other records that reference you.

After this process runs, we log that erasure was completed using only an anonymized identifier (a hash), not your email or name. That gives us an audit trail for compliance without retaining personal data.

## How to request erasure

You can request deletion of your account and data at any time from the app:

1. Go to **Settings**.
2. Open the **Danger zone** section.
3. Click **Delete account** and follow the confirmation steps.

After you confirm, your account is scheduled for permanent deletion. Once the scheduled date passes, our system will run the erasure process described above. You do not need to contact us separately unless you have a specific question.

## Contact for data-related requests

If you have questions about your data, want to exercise your rights (e.g. access or erasure), or want to report a concern, please contact us at the email address listed in the app or on our website. We will respond within a reasonable time.
